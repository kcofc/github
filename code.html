Spix's Macaw

About Spix's Macaw

The Spix's Macaw (the Little Blue Macaw) is by far the rarest Macaw in the world with no known wild specimens remaining. It is closely related to Glaucous Macaws,Lear's Macaws and the Hyacinth.All members of the Macaw group or either exticnt or close to being exticnt forcing them to be placed as Critically Endangered, and possibly exticnt.

Features

Spix's Macaws are medium-sized parrots weighing about 11 oz.Their beaks are black. Their fur is blue and around their eyes and beak is white.Theirtails are a black.

  • Average Mass: 12.69oz

Habitat

It was once understood that Spix's Macaws prefer areas with groves of buriti palms (Mauritia flexuosa) because their deit includes nuts producded by these palms.However, before their number dewindled,the birds were observed in the Juazeiro/Curaco area which is an arid region of northeast Brazil called the Tabebuia caraiba woodlands, where few palms can be found.

The birds also favored the dead crowns of craiberia trees as perches which suggests that these are important nest sites for Spix's Macaws.

Countries that had Large Spix's Macaw Popuations

Spix's Macaws were first found in the interior northwestern Brazil in small areas in southern Piaui,extreme southern Maranhoa, northestern Goias, and northwestern Bahia. 4 Birds now exist in Spain, Brazil, Qatar, and Nothern Switzerland.

Countries that had Small Spix's Macaw Populations

Spix's Macaws were rarely spoted in places such as the United States, Japan, Portugal, and Yugoslavia.
